# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers drive user-centered design through research, wireframes, and prototypes. They ensure the product is intuitive and accessible, collaborating closely with Product Managers and Developers.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and testing
- Design wireframes, user flows, and hi-fidelity prototypes
- Communicate design intent to developers
- Advocate for accessibility and usability standards

### Goals
- Deliver solutions that are intuitive and delightful for users
- Validate features before development via rapid prototyping

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews
- User testing reports
- Close collaboration in sprint ceremonies

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate the Agile process, ensuring effective ceremonies, removing impediments, and encouraging continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Run ceremonies (standups, sprint planning/retros)
- Remove blockers for the team
- Coach in Agile principles
- Track action items and impediments

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and engagement
- Foster a safe, collaborative environment

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups
- Retrospective summaries
- Direct support to team members

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the quality strategy, coordinate testing efforts, and manage defect reporting and release readiness.

### Responsibilities
- Develop/maintain test plans and strategies
- Automate tests where feasible
- Coordinate release validation and smoke testing
- Ensure test environments are well managed

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality releases with minimal defects
- Improve automation coverage over time

### Typical Communication
- Test summaries and defect reports
- Coordination with Developers and Product Managers

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts capture, clarify, and communicate requirements, acting as a bridge between business needs and technical implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Gather/document requirements from stakeholders
- Model business processes and workflows
- Write acceptance criteria, user stories, or specifications
- Facilitate workshops or walkthroughs

### Goals
- Minimize requirement ambiguity
- Speed up agreement on feature scope and value

### Typical Communication
- Requirement/user story docs
- Stakeholder meetings
- Clarification during planning and review

---

## Interactions With Existing Roles

- **UX Designers**: Collaborate with Product Managers to validate features; work with Developers to move from design to implementation; QA to verify usability.
- **Scrum Masters**: Remove blockers for Developers and QA; coordinate with Project Managers on schedules and risks.
- **QA Leads**: Partner with Developers and Product Managers on acceptance criteria; coordinate releases with Project Managers.
- **Business Analysts**: Bridge between stakeholders and Product/Project Managers; clarify requirements for Developers and QA.

---

> **These definitions help onboard new team members quickly and ensure clarity in cross-team work.**
