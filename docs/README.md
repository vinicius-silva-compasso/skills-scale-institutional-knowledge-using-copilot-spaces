## Project Management Processes Summary

OctoAcme leverages a structured, principle-driven approach to project management, emphasizing customer value, clear ownership, and iterative delivery. Every project begins with a robust initiation phase, including completion of a Project One-pager that articulates the problem, goals, key stakeholders, and success metrics. Planning activities then turn approved initiatives into actionable backlogs, define the Definition of Done, identify risks and dependencies, and establish a release and milestone map. Work execution is actively tracked using a project board with clearly defined workflow stages (Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, Done), and progress is shared in daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and regular demos or reviews.

Distinct roles and responsibilities underpin OctoAcme’s program processes. The Project Manager (PM) coordinates delivery, schedules, risks, and communications, ensuring teams deliver on scope and timeline. The Product Manager (PdM) crafts vision, prioritizes work, and defines outcomes, while Developers implement features, maintain high-quality code, and participate in reviews and design discussions. QA/test specialists validate quality against acceptance criteria, and stakeholders provide essential input and approvals. This role clarity is maintained across project documents and is essential for aligning on decisions and accountability.

Communication is frequent and multi-layered. The project team syncs daily or twice weekly as needed, with weekly PM and PdM alignment and monthly updates to stakeholders. Risks are proactively managed with a living risk register and documented escalation paths, moving from team-level triage to product leads and, if needed, sponsors for critical issues. Incident and stakeholder communication follows standardized templates to ensure clarity and transparency.

Quality assurance is built into the delivery process. All new logic is validated with unit, integration, and smoke tests, supported by CI automation for tests and security scanning. Manual QA steps gate critical releases, and quality signals (velocity, burndown, error rates, usage) are monitored on dashboards. Continuous improvement is institutionalized through post-milestone retrospectives and action item tracking, ensuring learnings are captured and applied to strengthen OctoAcme’s disciplined but agile project management methodology.

## For New Team Members

These documents are designed to give you clarity and practical guidance at each step of your work at OctoAcme.
- Start with the [Project Management Overview](./octoacme-project-management-overview.md) for the high-level lifecycle and role definitions.
- When joining a project, review the [Initiation Guide](./octoacme-project-initiation.md) and [Project Planning](./octoacme-project-planning.md) to understand how goals, work, and deliverables are set.
- Follow [Execution & Tracking](./octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) daily for team rhythm, workflow, and metrics.
- Use [Release & Deployment](./octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) to ship safely and consistently.
- Learn how we handle [Risks and Communication](./octoacme-risks-and-communication.md) and drive [Retrospectives & Improvements](./octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md).
- Reference [Roles and Personas](./octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) to clarify responsibilities and effective communication practices.
